# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## Overview
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime in which attackers trick users into authorizing payments or revealing sensitive banking details. With the rapid growth of digital payments in India, such frauds have increased significantly.

## Scenario (Fictional but Realistic)
Rahul, a college student, wanted to sell his old phone online. He listed it on a marketplace and was contacted by a “buyer” who agreed to pay via UPI. The buyer sent a QR code and asked Rahul to scan it to “receive” the payment.

## How the Fraud Happens (Step-by-Step)
1. The scammer contacts the victim pretending to be a buyer, seller, or bank official.  
2. They create urgency or trust (e.g., quick payment, limited-time offer).  
3. The victim is asked to scan a QR code or approve a “collect request.”  
4. Instead of receiving money, the victim unknowingly authorizes a payment.  
5. Money is instantly transferred to the scammer’s account.  

## Target Victims
- Students and first-time digital payment users  
- Elderly individuals unfamiliar with UPI systems  
- Online buyers/sellers on platforms like OLX  

## Consequences
Victims may lose significant amounts of money instantly, with little chance of recovery. It can also lead to stress, loss of trust in digital platforms, and potential misuse of personal data if additional details are shared.

## Conclusion
Awareness is the key to prevention. Users should remember that scanning a QR code or approving a request sends money, not receives it.
